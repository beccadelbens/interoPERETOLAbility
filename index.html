interoPERETOLAbility

Explore the ancient Peretola through technology

This website hosts and end of course project for a laboratory class at the University of Bologna.
The aim of this project is to integrate different types of technologies in order to analyze and represent some sort of cultural objects.

In this case I decided to start from a collection of photos of the "borgo" in which I live, Peretola (Florence). These photos were collected from Facebook platform and then analyzed and represented under different points of view.
The main purpose was to make these photos interoperable across different types of technologies.

3D modelling

Through the use of Blender I modelled a gallery of photos of the ancient Peretola. After the modelling the gallery was uploaded online on the p3d.in platform. The model can be browsed in the box below.

Digital History

In this section the photos were collected in a digital archive on the Omeka.net platform. Whenever possible each photo have been enriched with its metadata, such as title, author, description, etc. Omeka platform make use of Dublin Core elements to represent data. An important thing to observe is that for each photo was created a unique identifier through the use of the IIIF standard (that will be discussed in the third section).
The archive can be seen here.

Once properly represented the photos, they were geolocated in space through the use of another platform, HistoryPin. This platform allows users to locate the photos in the place in which they were taken. In this way you can see the differences between the past and the present.
The result can be seen here.

XML Technologies - IIIF

In this section of the project the photos were associated to a IIIF URI and were described through a IIIF manifest.
As first step I uploaded the photos on a hosting platform. This platform processed the photos associating them a IIIF URI. The links below allow you to access the photos and modify them directly online.
